COP 21 Climate Summit’s Momentum Keeps Going at the 2016 Investor Summit

February 1, 2016 - 2:45pm

Addthis

The 2016 Investor Summit on Climate Risk was the first major event on climate change for investors and businesses to discuss the far-reaching implications of the Paris Agreement. Ceres, the United Nations Foundation, and the United Nations Office for Partnerships co-hosted the Summit at the United Nations in NYC.

Sanjiv Malhotra
Director, Clean Energy Investment Center

In last week’s introductory post, we listed our first-year priorities for the Clean Energy Investment Center (CEIC). One of the primary focus areas is to engage investors and innovators locally and nationally. Last week, we stayed true to that goal by attending the 2016 Investor Summit on Climate Risk – the first of many stops that are planned in the coming months.

We wanted to share two key takeaways from this year’s Investor Summit. First and foremost, through our conversations, we were encouraged to find that investors are truly interested and eager to learn more about this investment space, and there is a significant amount of capital available for investment in clean energy in various asset classes. It was evident that the United Nations’ COP 21 climate summit was still fresh on the minds of all in attendance, and it is maintaining its impact. The cornerstone agreement reached at COP 21 was explicitly recognized through the announcement of a public-private Mission Innovation partnership, in which 20 nations, including the top five most populous, announced their intent to double public clean energy research and development spending over the next five years.

This milestone agreement has many excited about the demand for clean energy and the opportunity to make further progress on building a clean energy economy. Renewed optimism was evident at the 2016 Investor Summit about the possibilities that are within reach due to unprecedented global commitments and attention and interest from a new group of investors.

This new group of investors led to our second takeaway from the summit – there is a transition happening in the clean energy sector. In years past, venture capital firms conducted a large percentage of the clean energy investments. Now, as those firms redirect toward shorter-term areas, family foundations, pension funds, insurance firms, and larger corporations are gaining interest and beginning to enter energy investment. 

This transition is creating a need for firms and organizations to employ personnel in-house that understand the energy technology ecosystem and are capable of navigating this often complex environment. We learned that education is the most immediate need to advance investments, an area where the CEIC is uniquely qualified to assist. We can educate investors by connecting them with the top technical experts and with the most comprehensive project data on new and promising technologies. Additionally, we will offer a constant flow of information so firms will always be equipped with current data.

After attending the 2016 Investor Summit on Climate Risk, we are even more excited about the opportunities ahead. We, at the CEIC, are working diligently to conduct outreach and provide investors with the connections and resources they need to make informed decisions. Through continued collaboration, our aim is to maintain the interest and momentum happening in clean energy investments.

As always, stay tuned as we accelerate our efforts and keep you posted on our future activities. Do not hesitate to reach out at CEIC@hq.doe.gov.
