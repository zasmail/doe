CEIC Director Discusses Clean Energy Technology R&D at Duke University’s Fuqua School of Business EDGE Chats

October 5, 2016 - 3:32pm

Addthis

Sanjiv Malhotra, PhD, Director of the Clean Energy Investment Center at the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) talks about clean energy technology investment and the most promising areas of innovation in clean energy. Part of the EDGE Chats series of conversations with executives about leading issues and trends at the intersection of energy, environment, and business. Hosted by EDGE at Duke University's Fuqua School of Business.

Blog post from the Clean Energy Investment Center, October 5, 2016.

In a candid talk about the current state of clean energy technology investments, Sanjiv Malhotra, PhD, Director of the Clean Energy Investment Center (CEIC) details the role of the U.S. Department of Energy in designing and developing new mechanisms of finance to spur private sector capital into clean energy R&D. In this EDGE Chat, Dr. Malhotra discusses the most pressing challenges facing the world of cleantech investments and how CEIC is working to engage the private sector to invest in promising technologies that can be utilized in the short term while reducing carbon emissions in the long run. Don’t just take my word for it, check out the video!

The Clean Energy Investment Center is part of the Office of Technology Transitions, whose mission is to expand the commercial impact of DOE’s portfolio of Research, Development, Demonstration and Deployment (RDD&D) activities over the short, medium and long term.

Keywords: Cross-cutting materials, Micro-grid, Supply chain, Cleantech, Energy Storage, R&D
